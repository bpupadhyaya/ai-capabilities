Catalog Overview

Mission

This catalog exists to collect and organize every meaningful AI model ever created — from tiny 22M embedding models to trillion-parameter mixture-of-experts behemoths. The goal is a single, always-current reference that any developer can open on a Saturday afternoon and answer the question: "What is the best model I can run right now, on my hardware, privately, offline?"

We believe the most important breakthrough in AI is not the next GPT release — it is the moment a phone in someone's pocket becomes a fully capable reasoning agent that never phones home. On-device AGI means privacy is not a feature you pay extra for; it is the default. It means the intelligence gap between the richest and the poorest humans on earth collapses entirely.

Every entry in this catalog is cross-referenced with the hardware required to run it, the inference engines that support it, quantization formats available, and whether it can operate fully offline. The AGI Roadmap page maps known capability gaps and proposes a composition architecture for assembling general intelligence from open-source modules available today.
